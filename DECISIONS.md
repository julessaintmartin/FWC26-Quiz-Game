# FWC26 Quiz — Decisions Guide

This is the "what are we actually building and why" document. It walks through every
decision a viral, daily-updating quiz game needs to make, records the choices we
locked in, and flags the few you'll still want to weigh in on before launch.

---

## 1. The core concept

A web app that drops a **fresh quiz after every World Cup 2026 match day**, testing how
closely you watched. Play in ~90 seconds, get a score, share it, challenge a friend,
climb a leaderboard, come back tomorrow. The tournament runs **June 11 – July 19, 2026**,
which gives you a natural daily content engine for five weeks with zero writing effort
once the pipeline runs.

The whole product strategy rides on one loop:

> **Watch the match → play the quiz → share your score → a friend plays → repeat tomorrow.**

Everything below serves that loop.

---

## 2. Quiz format — *Recap trivia* ✅ (your choice)

Each day's quiz is multiple-choice questions about **what actually happened that match
day**: final scores, who won, goal counts, records, standout moments.

Why this works for virality:
- It's a **"did you actually watch?"** flex. People share to prove they paid attention.
- It's fast to play and instantly understandable — no rules to learn.
- It **only exists because the match happened**, so it's inherently timely and fresh.

Design rules we baked in:
- **10 questions**, mixed difficulty (easy openers → harder deep cuts).
- A short **explanation** after each answer, so even wrong answers feel rewarding and teach
  you something you can repeat to friends.
- Difficulty ramps so the average fan scores ~6–7/10 — high enough to feel good, low enough
  that a perfect score is brag-worthy.

---

## 3. The viral loop — *All three* ✅ (your choice)

You picked the full sharing stack. Here's how each piece works and why it matters:

**a) Shareable score card.** After finishing, the app generates a clean image
("I scored 9/10 on Match Day 8 🔥") sized for Instagram/X/WhatsApp stories. Uses the
native share sheet on mobile so it's one tap to post. *This is the single most important
growth feature* — it's the unit that travels.

**b) Leaderboard + streaks.** Global and daily rankings, plus a personal **day streak**
("🔥 5-day streak"). Streaks are the retention mechanic — they give people a reason to come
back every single match day, not just when they remember.

**c) Challenge a friend.** Any result can generate a head-to-head link. Your friend opens it,
plays the *same* quiz, and sees whether they beat you. This is the lowest-friction way to pull
a brand-new person into the app — it arrives as a personal dare, not an ad.

Together these cover all three growth jobs: **reach** (score card), **retention**
(leaderboard/streaks), and **invitation** (challenge links).

---

## 4. Build scope — *Full auto-updating* ✅ (your choice)

You asked for the real thing, not just a demo: a system that **generates a new quiz from
match results automatically**, every match day, with no manual writing.

How we deliver that:
- `generate_quiz.py` pulls the official-style fixture/result feed (see §6), finds the matches
  for a given date, and **writes the quiz JSON automatically**.
- A scheduler (cron or GitHub Actions) runs it each night so a new quiz is live by morning.
- The app always loads the **latest** quiz, so "going live after each match day" happens on
  its own.

What still needs a human-in-the-loop, and why it's fine:
- Result feeds reliably give **scores and winners**; richer details (exact goalscorer minute,
  "player of the match") depend on the data source. The generator writes solid
  score/result/standings questions automatically, and you can optionally hand-add 1–2 spicy
  questions per day in ~2 minutes if you want extra flavour. This is documented in the README.

---

## 5. Tech stack — *single self-contained web app* ✅ (we chose, since you said "you decide")

**Front end: one `index.html` file.** Plain HTML/CSS/JS, no build step, no framework.

Why not React/Next? For a quiz that needs to (a) load instantly on a phone, (b) be hosted
anywhere for free, and (c) be dead-simple to hand off or hack on, a single file is the fastest
path to live and the easiest to keep alive for five weeks. There's nothing here that a
framework would meaningfully simplify. If this grows into a year-round product with accounts
and many game modes, that's the moment to migrate to React — not before.

**Data: static JSON files** (`quizzes/*.json`) generated by the pipeline. No database needed
to serve quizzes — they're just files on a CDN, which is infinitely scalable and free.

**Leaderboard storage:** see §7 — this is the one real decision left.

---

## 6. Data source — openfootball (free, no key) ✅ (we chose)

We use the **openfootball/worldcup.json** public-domain dataset for the 2026 schedule and
results. It needs **no API key**, costs nothing, and has no rate limits, which removes the most
common reason side-projects die (a paid API bill or an expired key).

Trade-off: it updates a bit slower than commercial live-score APIs and carries scores/winners
but not always granular events. We judged free + zero-maintenance worth more than real-time
granularity for a once-a-day quiz. The generator is written so you can **swap in a richer paid
API** (API-Football, Sportmonks, BALLDONTLIE) later by changing one function — documented in
`generate_quiz.py`.

---

## 7. The one decision still open: where the leaderboard lives

A *global* leaderboard needs a tiny backend to store scores. We built the app so it works
**immediately with on-device storage** (your scores, streaks, and personal best persist in the
browser), and we left a **clean, commented hook** to switch on a real shared leaderboard when
you're ready. Your options:

| Option | Effort | Global leaderboard? | Cost | Best when |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **On-device only** (default, shipping now) | none | No — personal scores/streaks only | $0 | Launch today, validate the loop |
| **Supabase** (recommended next) | ~30 min | Yes | $0 free tier | You see traction and want real rankings |
| **Firebase** | ~30 min | Yes | $0 free tier | You already use Google's stack |
| **Cloudflare KV / Workers** | ~1 hr | Yes | $0 free tier | You want it all on one CDN |

The README has copy-paste Supabase setup. **Recommendation:** launch on-device today, wire
Supabase the moment the score-card sharing shows signs of life. Don't build the backend before
you know people share — that's the classic order-of-operations mistake.

---

## 8. Things to decide before you scale (not blockers for launch)

- **Branding/name.** "FWC26 Quiz" is a placeholder. A memorable name + handle helps the share
  card travel. (Note: avoid official FIFA marks/logos to stay clear of trademark issues — lean
  on a generic football aesthetic, which is what the app uses.)
- **Accounts vs. anonymous.** We default to anonymous + a display name (lowest friction, best
  for virality). Add real accounts only if you need cross-device streaks.
- **Monetisation.** Out of scope for launch. If it pops, the clean paths are a sponsor on the
  score card or a tip jar — not ads, which would kill the share aesthetic.
